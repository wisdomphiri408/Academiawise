What is the difference between a value and a fact?

Facts are objective statements drowned through empirical methods. values on the other hand, aresubjective statements depend on ones perspectives. For example, someone might say the water is cold and the other might say it is just right.

Why is it important for scientists to differentiate between facts and values in their research?

It is important because it helps them from beingbias and forming conclusions depending on their personal beliefs.

How can scientific research help in shaping or informing personal values?

Scientific research provides evidence that can support or challenge personal values. For instance, research showing that smoking causes serious health issues can reinforce the value that smoking is harmful.

What happens to scientific facts when new data or methods become available?

Science is dynamic and self-correcting. As new data and methods emerge, previous facts may be revised or replaced with more accurate information.
